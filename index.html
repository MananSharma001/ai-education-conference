Why Attend

This conference brings together students and professionals to explore the complex governance challenges around AI in education. We’ll look beyond technical development to the ethical dilemmas, cultural considerations, and policy decisions that transform learning environments.

What to Expect

  • Interactive workshop with live Mentimeter polling and rapid scenarios
  • Expert panel sharing real-world governance decisions across K-12 & Higher Ed
  • Actionable frameworks for evaluating AI tools and policies

Who Should Attend

Computer Science & International Affairs students, educators, researchers, and policy professionals.

Expert Panel & Q&A

Real cases, real decisions—practical guidance for institutions and classrooms.

Panel Title

Shaping the Future: Stakeholder Voices on AI Education Governance

Format

Moderated discussion with audience Q&A and live polling.

Confirmed Panelists

  • Josef Oduwo — CIO, Victorian Curriculum & Assessment Authority; Vice-Chair, ACS Victoria
  • Trinity McNicol — Associate Director, Data & AI (KPMG Australia); former Senior Lead, Privacy & Data Governance
  • Kenea (Nea) Dhillon — Director, AI Technology & Delivery, Victoria University (Workshop Facilitator)

Key Themes

Workshop and panel weave these threads throughout:

Governance

Who decides how AI is used? Balancing innovation with risk; engaging stakeholders.

Cultural Adaptation

Designing AI that respects diverse educational values; global vs local tensions.

Policy Implementation

Bridging policy intentions and classroom reality; change management; meaningful metrics.

Ethics

Student privacy and data rights; algorithmic fairness; preserving human agency.

Participant Outcomes

By attending, you’ll leave with actionable skills, new frameworks, and stronger awareness of how AI governance impacts education.

Knowledge Gains

  • Critical Thinking: Evaluate AI proposals beyond their technical features.
  • Stakeholder Awareness: Understand the voices of students, teachers, policy makers & industry.
  • Ethical Reasoning: Analyze dilemmas of privacy, fairness & bias in education contexts.

Practical Takeaways

  • Frameworks for assessing AI tools and institutional policies.
  • Career insights into ed-tech, policy, and governance roles.
  • Networking with peers & experts in governance and AI.
